# AI Change Log — 2026-06-22-change-24

## Reason

Phase 1 of v0.4 introduces the streaming audio buffer and streaming transcriber
components. These lay the groundwork for low-latency incremental transcription
by processing audio chunks in a background thread instead of waiting for a full
recording to finish.

## Files changed

- `src/audio/streaming_buffer.py` — new `StreamingAudioBuffer` class.
  - Thread-safe append/commit/clear/get_all/get_unprocessed_audio.
  - Tracks processed/unprocessed sample boundaries.
  - Duration helpers for total and unprocessed audio.
- `src/asr/streaming.py` — new `StreamingTranscriber` class.
  - Background thread loop consuming audio from an internal
    `StreamingAudioBuffer`.
  - Runs VAD on chunks and transcribes speech with an injected `ASRProvider`.
  - Queues `TranscriptionResult`s with `is_final=False` for partial results.
  - Marks the last partial result final after a configurable silence pause.
  - Flushes remaining audio as a final result on `stop()`.
- `tests/unit/test_streaming_buffer.py` — unit tests for `StreamingAudioBuffer`
  including thread-safety coverage.
- `tests/unit/test_streaming_transcriber.py` — unit tests for
  `StreamingTranscriber` using mocked `ASRProvider` and `VADProvider`.
- `docs/architecture.md` — documented the new streaming buffer and transcriber
  modules and updated the v0.4 roadmap status.

## Risk

- Low-to-moderate. New code is isolated in separate modules and is not yet wired
  into `App`, so existing dictation behavior is unchanged.
- Timing-sensitive tests use small sleeps; they may be flaky on heavily loaded
  machines, but timeouts and short intervals keep runtimes well under the
  60-second limit.
- The streaming transcriber is mock-friendly and does not instantiate real ASR
  models or VAD backends in unit tests.
